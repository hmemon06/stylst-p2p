## The App: Stylst AI

**What it is:** An AI-powered outfit rater. You take a photo (or upload one) of your fit and the AI gives you a score out of 100, a vibe label (e.g. "Quiet Luxury Aligned", "Chaos Energy", "Sleeper Hit"), and four subscores:
- **Aura** — overall energy / impression
- **Fit** — how well pieces work structurally
- **Palette** — color coordination
- **Trend** — alignment with current fashion zeitgeist

**The Glow Up (hero feature):** AI redesigns your outfit into its best possible version and re-rates it. Shows side-by-side: Current vs. Glow Up. The "Potential Score" shows how high you could go.

**Context Modes** (affects how the AI judges): Roast Me · Date · Office · Gym · Party

**Style DNA Onboarding:** A 2-min quiz that builds a personalized style profile. Covers aesthetic identity (Y2K, Streetwear, Old Money, Gorpcore, Coquette, Grunge, etc.), color palette, fit preferences, and goals.

**Monetization:**
- Free trial (limited scans)
- Subscription (via Stripe/Superwall) unlocks unlimited scans
- Redesign credits (IAP via RevenueCat) — buy packs of 3 / 10 / 25

**Shareable score cards:** Score stamp + vibe label + stats grid, styled like a fashion editorial.

**Aesthetic / Brand Voice:** Dark, cinematic UI. Bodoni Moda + Satoshi fonts. Feels like a fashion magazine crossed with a hype app. Brutally honest but fun. Not corporate — more like your most stylish friend who doesn't sugarcoat.

## General UGC Rules (apply to all formats)

- **Never open with the app name or a product pitch** — earn attention first
- **Tone:** entertaining first, informative second, promotional third
- The app should feel like a natural payoff, not the point
- No stock-feeling copy ("Are you tired of...")
- Don't make it feel like a tutorial — feel like content, not an ad
- Lowercase, conversational copy reads more native than title case
- Keep each slide focused on one single idea

## Content Types

---

### 1. Listicle / Micro-habits

**Format:** "X things that did Y for me" or "X ways to do Z"

**Vibe:** Personal, reflective, low-key vulnerable. Feels like someone sharing what actually worked — not a top-10 article. The example format that performs well:

```
[Hook slide]
"5 micro-habits that helped me go from fashion insecure to style confident:"

[Each numbered slide — 3-line rhythm:]
Line 1 → number + bold statement (the habit/tip)
Line 2 → the before / the problem (why it mattered)
Line 3 → the payoff / what changed

[CTA slide]
soft close — "save this", "follow for pt 2", "link in bio"
```

**Promo placement:** Slide 2 or 3 of 5–6. Frame Stylst as the tool that enabled the habit — not the point of the video.

**Example promo slide:**
```
2. i started getting brutally honest feedback

i needed someone to tell me the truth without sugarcoating it

i use the stylst app now — it's an ai fashion critic that rates
outfits 1-100 and actually tells you what works
```

**Hook formulas to riff on:**
- `5 micro-habits that helped me go from [pain state] to [desired state]:`
- `5 things that quietly transformed my style (save this)`
- `5 habits i wish i had in my early 20s (style edition)`
- `5 things i stopped doing that changed how i dress`
- `i used to hate getting dressed. then i changed 5 things.`

**Concept bank:**

| Concept | Hook | Stylst placement |
|---|---|---|
| Micro-habits | "5 micro-habits that helped me go from fashion insecure to style confident" | Habit #2 — getting honest feedback |
| Style glow up | "5 things i changed that made people notice my style" | Tip #3 — using AI to validate fits before leaving |
| Closet clarity | "5 habits that made me actually love my wardrobe" | Tip #2 — rating every outfit, knowing what works |
| Confidence dressing | "5 things nobody told me about dressing with confidence" | Tip #3 — honest feedback without the social filter |
| Anti-trend | "5 habits i picked up that made me stop chasing trends" | Tip #2 — learning your actual score and style DNA |

### 2. "X outfits I wore this week"

**Format:** Day-by-day outfit diary. Personal and relatable. Works as a soft showcase.

**Vibe:** Casual, like a friend sharing their week. Not curated-to-death. Realness is the point.

```
[Hook slide]
"5 outfits i wore this week (feat. my AI score for each)"

[One slide per outfit:]
Line 1 → day or occasion (monday · date night · back to office)
Line 2 → outfit description or vibe label from Stylst
Line 3 → score or AI roast line

[CTA slide]
"app is called stylst. rates your fits 1-100. try it free."
```

**Hook formulas:**
- `5 outfits i wore this week + my AI score for each`
- `i wore something different every day this week. here's what the AI thought.`
- `rating my week of outfits with an AI (it was honest)`
- `5 outfits, 5 occasions, 5 scores — this week in fits`

### 3. "1 piece X ways" / Wardrobe math

**Format:** Outfit formulas built around a single hero piece. Practical, shareable, saves-friendly.

**Vibe:** Styling content. Less personal story, more "use this." High save rate.

```
[Hook slide]
"1 jacket. 5 outfits. zero new pieces."

[One slide per look:]
Line 1 → the occasion or vibe
Line 2 → the combination (jacket + white tee + wide-leg trousers)
Line 3 → optional: Stylst score or AI verdict on the combo

[CTA slide]
"stylst rates every outfit 1-100. so you know before you leave the house."
```

**Hook formulas:**
- `1 jacket 5 ways (from gym to dinner)`
- `i wore the same white tee 6 different ways this week`
- `3 pieces. 7 outfits. here's the math.`
- `the only 5 outfit formulas you actually need`
- `i built a week of outfits from 4 pieces. here's how.`

### 4. POV / First-person story

**Format:** "POV: [relatable scenario]" — single narrative arc across slides. Feels most like organic TikTok content.

**Vibe:** Storytelling. One slide per beat. Builds to a reveal or realization. App enters as a natural plot point.

```
[Hook slide]
"POV: you let an AI roast your outfit"

[Story beats — one per slide]
Beat 1 → set the scene / the before
Beat 2 → the inciting moment
Beat 3 → app enters as the pivot
Beat 4 → the result / revelation
Beat 5 → CTA or reflection
```

**Hook formulas:**
- `POV: you let an AI roast your outfit`
- `POV: you find out your "signature style" is a 4/10`
- `POV: AI tells you what your friends were too polite to say`
- `i asked AI to be honest about my style. this is what it said.`
- `30 days of letting AI rate my outfits. here's what changed.`

---

### 5. "Signs / Green flags / Red flags"

**Format:** Quick-hit list of observations. No narrative — just punchy truths. High shareability.

**Vibe:** Bold, slightly provocative. Works as a "tag someone" post.

```
[Hook slide]
"signs your style is actually leveling up (most people miss these)"

[One sign per slide — 1-2 lines max:]
"you care more about fit than brand"
"you dress for yourself, not for the caption"
"you know what actually looks good on you vs. what's just trending"

[Stylst slide — 1 sign that references the app naturally:]
"you get objective feedback — not just 'omg cute'"
(small: "i use stylst for this. ai rates my fits without the social filter.")

[CTA]
```

**Hook formulas:**
- `signs your style is quietly leveling up`
- `green flags in someone's wardrobe`
- `things people with good style do differently`
- `red flags in your own closet (i had all of these)`
- `signs you're finally dressing for yourself`
